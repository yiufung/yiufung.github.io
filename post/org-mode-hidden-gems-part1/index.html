Org-mode Hidden Gems

10 May 2019

I have been using Org for a year now, and it never fails to impress me with its versatility. Recently I started to read Org-mode manual, and it surprises me there are so many useful options/commands I didn’t know before, so I’d like to share some of them here.

Each option/command is accompanied with a GIF to demonstrate its usefulness. Settings are based on my preference, but a man’s Emacs is his own castle, so do customize it for your own good. When in doubt, C-h v and C-h f are always your friends.

Avoid accidentally editing texts in invisible area

When text is inserted near invisible area (...), I’m never sure what exactly is changed. Usually I would expand to check, only to end up losing my focus during panic TAB ing.

Set org-catch-invisible-edits to error to prevent editing invisible area, showing an error message in echo area instead. I prefer show-and-error, which automatically expand and move focus apart from the notice.

Show headings only

Usually I would have some texts under a tree right before its subtrees. This could be visually disturbing when I TAB through trying to glance at tree structure. All I want is the headings. In this case, org-kill-note-or-show-branches (bound to C-c C-k) comes in handy.

Clone trees with time shifts

Bound to C-c C-x c, org-clone-subtree-with-time-shift create multiple subtrees with timestamps shifted (+3d in the example). Useful for creating tree structures for repeated tasks in the future.

In collapsed view, hide empty lines between subtrees

Set org-cycle-separator-lines to 0. Gives a more compact and consistent view, especially in a large Org file.

Turn plain list into subtrees

org-list-make-subtree (bound to C-c C-*).
